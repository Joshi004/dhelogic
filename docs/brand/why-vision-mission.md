# Our Why — The Story Behind TechSergy

*The vision, the mission, and the conviction that started it all.*

---

## A note from the founder

For about ten years before TechSergy, I was an engineer in the industry. Like every good engineering team I worked with, I was raised on a simple discipline: **don't repeat yourself, optimize the system, and don't over-engineer.** You reach for the *right* tool for the problem in front of you — no more, no less. That discipline isn't bureaucracy. It's respect: for the user, for the budget, for the people who have to maintain the thing after you.

Then large, general-purpose AI models arrived — GPT, Claude, and the rest — and something shifted in how we build.

These models are genuinely remarkable. They're powerful enough to do almost anything, and that power is intoxicating. We became so impressed by how *easy* it had become to generate a solution that we quietly stopped asking the question every engineer used to ask first: **what does this actually cost?** Not just in dollars, but in energy, in dependency, in control.

That unasked question is why TechSergy exists.

---

## The bazooka and the mosquito

Here is the idea at the center of everything we do.

You don't use a bazooka to kill a mosquito in your living room. You don't use a sword to chop vegetables for dinner. Both tools are perfectly *capable* of doing the job — but neither is the *right* tool, and using them anyway costs you. Financially. Practically. Sometimes socially.

Reaching for a massive frontier model to solve a small, well-defined problem is the same mistake. It works, so we tell ourselves it's fine. But "it works" was never the bar good engineering held itself to. The bar was: *is this the right tool, at the right cost, that we can rely on and control?*

It gets personal for me when I see it up close:

- An engineer prompting an LLM to **generate a simple JSON object** that a few lines of conventional code would produce — faster, cheaper, and deterministically.
- A team calling a frontier model to **parse a structured PDF**, when ordinary software has solved structured extraction reliably for years.
- Companies routing **mundane, high-volume tasks** — classification, information extraction, routing, tagging — through the most expensive, most general models available, paying premium frontier prices for work a small specialized model does just as well.

None of this is anyone's fault, and that matters to how we talk about it.

---

## We understand *why* it happens

I don't say any of this to scold engineers or companies. I've been in the room. I know the pressures.

Business demands speed. Teams are asked to deliver value as fast as possible, and a powerful general model that solves *everything* on day one is the rational shortcut when the clock is running. Building a fine-tuned, distilled model that matches GPT or Claude on one specific task is real, focused, research-heavy work — and for most teams, at that moment, it is simply **not** where they can afford to spend their energy.

So companies make a completely understandable trade: they ship the bazooka, because the bazooka is sitting right there and the deadline is today.

The cost of that trade doesn't disappear, though. It just moves downstream — into next quarter's inference bill, into a growing dependency on a third party, into data leaving your walls, into an environmental footprint nobody is accounting for. Someone has to do the regress, the research, and the careful engineering to recover that cost.

**That someone is us.** It's the one thing we do, and it's the thing we're experts at.

## Why TechSergy exists (our mission)

> **We help companies stop overpaying for AI — by replacing oversized, general-purpose inference with right-sized systems that match the quality you depend on, cost a fraction to run, and stay fully under your control.**

We are a team of AI research engineers and systems engineers with years of experience, including backgrounds from institutions like the IITs and organizations like Apple. We have spent our careers on exactly this kind of problem: taking a working-but-wasteful system and making it efficient without losing what made it valuable.

When you bring us in, the work looks like this:

1. **We question the spend.** We start by asking whether you genuinely need to spend what you're spending on production inference today. Often, a large part of it is the bazooka tax.
2. **We find the optimization.** We examine your real production pipeline — the process *and* the models — and identify where cost is hiding and where it can be structurally removed.
3. **We estimate the savings honestly.** Before you commit to anything, we put a number on the overall cost we can realistically save you.
4. **We build the right tools for *your* job.** We guide you through the transition: fine-tuning, distillation, quantization, and self-hosted inference — creating models sized to your actual problem rather than to the whole world's.

## What you get back (beyond the savings)

Cost reduction is the headline, but it isn't the whole story. When you replace a rented frontier model with a system built for your task, you get back things you'd quietly given away:

- **You own the intelligence.** The models, the artifacts, the configuration — they're yours to run and maintain. You're no longer renting your core capability from someone else.
- **You control the cost.** Predictable, projectable spend instead of a meter that runs faster as you grow successful.
- **Your data stays yours.** When your requirements call for it, inference runs inside your own environment. Sensitive data no longer has to travel to a third party's servers — which matters enormously in regulated fields like healthcare, finance, and legal.
- **You hold the steering wheel.** Full control and predictability over the system your business runs on.

## The line we will not cross: quality

Here is the single most important thing, and the place most cost-cutting goes wrong.

**No business wants to compromise on quality, and neither do we.** A cheaper system that's worse isn't a saving — it's a liability with a discount. So we treat quality as a non-negotiable, and we don't ask you to take that on faith.

We prove it. Not theoretically — with **actual data and benchmarks**. We measure your current system on real production examples first, and we only move an optimized system into production once it meets the quality bar you've agreed to. **Evidence first, claims second.** If we can't prove the quality holds, it doesn't ship.

## Our vision

A world where companies use AI **deliberately** — where the size of the model fits the size of the problem, where intelligence is owned rather than rented, and where "it works" is held to the older, better standard: *the right tool, at the right cost, fully under your control, with the quality proven.*

We want to make the optimized path the obvious path — so that choosing the bazooka for the mosquito becomes the strange decision, not the default one.

---

## What we believe (our principles)

- **The right tool for the problem.** Capability is not the same as fitness. We optimize for the problem actually in front of you.
- **Measured, not promised.** We benchmark before we propose and prove before we ship.
- **Built to hand over.** We give you the artifacts, configuration, and documentation to run the system yourself. Your independence is the goal, not your dependence on us.
- **Your data, your environment.** Privacy and control are features, not afterthoughts.
- **Savings that last.** We design for durable, structural cost reduction — not a one-time patch that erodes the moment your traffic or the model landscape shifts.
- **Cost is never just financial.** Every wasteful system also carries an environmental and strategic cost. Removing waste is the responsible thing, not only the cheaper one.
